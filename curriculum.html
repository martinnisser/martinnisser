Curriculum

Web Design I

Taught in both English and Spanish, Web Design I is designed to teach our students the fundamentals of web programming, building self-efficacy and digital literacy in the process. It has three main parts: core technical skills, career-readiness, and a capstone project.

Core technical skills
The curriculum teaches participants the fundamentals of HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Topics include Bootstrap, flexbox, responsive design, branching statements, loops, and functions.

Career-readiness
The class includes dedicated lectures on presentation preparation and public speaking. Students are also introduced to relevant technology-facing career paths by guest lecturers.

Capstone project
Students apply the taught material to build a website centered on addressing challenges to affect social impact in their communities. Common topics include domestic violence and addiction.

Sample capstone projects

Project websites from students have covered a variety of issues including domestic violence, addiction, parole reform, gun violence, and poverty. Students research their topic of choice and develop websites that either display information and resources or represent a future or existing organization. WhyNow

Women in Recovery

WhyNow was launched during our Summer '21 cohort by three incarcerated women from the Maine Department of Corrections. They describe their mission as follows: "Our mission is to do our best to provide women just recently released from prison or incarceration with any resources we have found helpful or believe will be of help to you along your own journey through recovery. You can be successful in both reacclamating to a healthy, balanced lifestyle and maintain your sobriety while you also face the underlying challenges that may have led you down the wrong path once before."

Stop Domestic Violence

There's Always Help

Stop Domestic Violence was launched during our Summer '21 cohort by two incarcerated women from Massachusetts. They describe their mission as follows: "Our mission statement is to increase awareness of Domestic Violence and provide resources to support victims of abuse, so they can lead a better life."

Hey Sister!

Awareness to Action

Hey Sister! was launched during our Summer '21 cohort by a group of five incarcerated women from the Maine Department of Corrections. They describe their mission as follows: "We seek to create awareness of the challenges of incarcerated and formerly incarcerated women. We illuminate the hardship of mental health and substance abuse, the hurdles to education, the unrelenting discrimination, and the outdated legislation we face every day. We seek to turn awareness into action."
